NYC Schools' Absentee Rate Is Still Higher Than It Was Pre-Pandemic

In several school districts, more than half of the student body was chronically absent last year.

By Liya Cui
December 20, 2023

Schools across the country have been struggling to retain students since the pandemic started, including New York City public schools. A student is considered chronically absent if they have missed at least 18 school days, which is 10% of the school year. The percentage of chronically absent students was around 25% before the pandemic and has since reached between 30-40%. While some aspects of our lives have "returned to normal" since 2020, this increase indicates that some issues in public education have remained.

Members of Community Education Council District 5, whose school district had the highest number of chronically absent students last year at 58%, did not respond to requests for comment.
